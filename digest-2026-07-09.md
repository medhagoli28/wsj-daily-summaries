**1. 3 Questions to Ask Yourself About Your First 401(k)** *(advice — underlying topic)* — When opening a first 401(k), advisers say the three questions that matter most are whether you're contributing enough to capture the full employer match, whether to choose Roth (after-tax) or traditional (pre-tax) contributions, and how your money is invested and vested. Not contributing enough to earn a match — commonly a 100% match up to 4%, or 50% up to 6% — effectively leaves free compensation on the table. The Roth-versus-traditional choice hinges on whether you expect a higher tax rate in retirement than today, since Roth withdrawals are tax-free while traditional contributions defer taxes now. Vesting schedules matter too: your own contributions are always 100% yours, but employer matches may take years to fully vest, so leaving early can forfeit that money. *Sources: [Indeed](https://www.indeed.com/career-advice/pay-salary/401-k-questions-to-ask), [Charles Schwab](https://www.schwab.com/learn/story/401k-match), [Empower](https://www.empower.com/the-currency/work/how-does-401k-matching-work).*

**2. A Couple Living on One Income Wants to Buy a Home. Can They Afford It?** *(advice — underlying topic)* — The standard benchmark for single-income home affordability in 2026 remains the 28/36 rule: keep total housing costs (principal, interest, taxes, insurance and HOA fees) at or below 28% of gross monthly income, and all debt payments under 36%. There is no fixed minimum income to qualify — lenders instead weigh debt-to-income ratio, employment stability, credit score and loan type, with most conventional loans requiring a credit score of at least 620 and down payments ranging from as little as 0% (VA) or 3.5% (FHA) to conventional minimums. Because one earner carries the entire mortgage, advisers stress building a robust emergency fund to cover repairs or a loss of income. The rules of thumb are only starting points: buyers should evaluate their full financial picture and whether a payment will still feel comfortable years down the road. *Sources: [Opendoor](https://www.opendoor.com/articles/calculate-your-mortgage-affordability), [Loan Pronto](https://loanpronto.com/blog/buying-a-home-on-one-income-2026-guide/), [Freedom Mortgage](https://www.freedommortgage.com/learn/homebuying/percentage-income-mortgage).*
