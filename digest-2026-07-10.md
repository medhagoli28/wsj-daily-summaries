## 💻 Tech

**1. China Successfully Launches Reusable Rocket in a Win for Space Program** — China successfully recovered the first stage of its Long March-10B carrier rocket during an orbital launch on Friday, July 10, 2026, a breakthrough as it races to catch up with SpaceX in reusable rocket technology. Launched from Hainan island in southern China, the first-stage booster returned to a floating maritime platform roughly six minutes after separating from the upper stage. The mission marked the world's first successful recovery of a carrier rocket using a net-capture system and China's first-ever maritime recovery of a rocket booster; unlike SpaceX rockets that use deployable landing legs, the Long March-10B shifts much of the recovery function to ground-based infrastructure. Reusable technology can sharply lower the cost and turnaround time of launches, bringing China closer to matching American firms on capabilities seen as critical to space exploration and satellite infrastructure. *Sources: [CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2026/07/10/china/china-reusable-rockets-test-breakthrough-intl-hnk), [Space.com](https://www.space.com/space-exploration/launches-spacecraft/making-history-china-lands-rocket-during-an-orbital-launch-for-1st-time-ever), [CGTN](https://news.cgtn.com/news/2026-07-10/China-achieves-reusable-rocket-breakthrough-with-Long-March-10B-1OF9e3z0u2Y/p.html).*

**2. Record Companies Push to Label AI Songs on Streaming Platforms** — A coalition led by the RIAA and the International Federation of the Phonographic Industry (IFPI) is pushing streaming platforms like Spotify and Apple Music to adopt a voluntary system labeling AI-made songs, joined by the Recording Academy, SAG-AFTRA, the Human Artistry Campaign, and the American Association of Independent Music. The plan proposes two tags: "AI-generated" for tracks built entirely by AI from a text prompt or where a machine produced the lead vocal or main instrumentals, and "AI-assisted" for human-driven tracks that lean on AI in places. Consumer demand is strong: a Deezer/Ipsos study of 9,000 listeners found 97% could not distinguish AI songs from human-made ones, yet 80% wanted fully AI tracks clearly labeled. Some platforms are already acting, with Spotify offering a voluntary AI-credit feature and Apple Music introducing optional "transparency tags" for AI-assisted music or artwork. *Sources: [Variety](https://variety.com/2026/music/news/ifpi-riaa-grammys-sag-aftra-labeling-program-ai-music-1236806377/), [Hollywood Reporter](https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/music/music-industry-news/riaa-pushes-for-ai-labels-on-streaming-services-1236643840/), [Music Business Worldwide](https://www.musicbusinessworldwide.com/record-industry-proposes-ai-labeling-system-for-streaming-platforms/).*

**3. Meta Failed to Protect Users From Addictive Apps, EU Says** — The European Commission issued a preliminary finding on July 10, 2026, that Meta may have breached the Digital Services Act by designing Facebook and Instagram to keep users hooked, singling out infinite scroll, autoplay, and push notifications that Brussels says should be switched off by default. The Commission argued Meta underestimated the effect these features have on users, particularly children and vulnerable adults, and that its existing time-management tools can be easily dismissed and fail to meaningfully reduce usage. If the Commission ultimately confirms a breach, Meta faces fines of up to 6% of its worldwide annual turnover, which amounts to roughly $12 billion. The findings are preliminary, and Meta will have the opportunity to respond before a final decision is reached. *Sources: [CNN Business](https://www.cnn.com/2026/07/10/tech/meta-eu-addictive-design), [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/10/eu-threatens-meta-with-fines-over-addictive-features-on-facebook-and-instagram/), [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/7/10/eu-says-addictive-features-on-instagram-and-facebook-breach-its-rules).*

**4. SK Hynix to Debut on Wall Street After Biggest Share Sale by Foreign Company** — South Korean chipmaker SK Hynix debuted on the Nasdaq after raising $26.51 billion through American depositary receipts, the largest-ever share sale by a non-U.S. company. The offering sold 177.9 million American depositary shares at $149 each, topping the $25 billion raised by Alibaba in 2014 and ranking as the second-biggest U.S. stock sale on record behind SpaceX's $75 billion offering the prior month. Demand was reportedly more than seven times the number of shares available, underscoring strong investor appetite for a key player in the AI supply chain. The massive buildout of AI data centers has fueled demand for the memory chips SK Hynix produces. *Sources: [CNN Business](https://www.cnn.com/2026/07/10/business/sk-hynix-us-listing-ai-chip-boom-intl-hnk), [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/economy/2026/7/10/south-koreas-sk-hynix-raises-26-5bn-in-record-breaking-us-ipo), [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-09/sk-hynix-is-said-to-price-us-share-offering-at-149-apiece-mrdz562z).*

**5. OpenAI's No. 2 Executive to Step Down in Latest Leadership Shake-Up** — Fidji Simo, OpenAI's No. 2 executive and CEO of Applications, announced on July 9, 2026, that she is stepping down from her full-time role and transitioning to a part-time advisory position, citing a severe exacerbation of postural orthostatic tachycardia syndrome (POTS), a chronic neuroimmune condition she was diagnosed with in 2019. Simo joined OpenAI's board in 2024 and came aboard full-time in May 2025 in the newly created CEO of Applications role reporting directly to Sam Altman, after leading Instacart through its 2023 IPO and previously running the Facebook app at Meta. Her departure leaves Altman searching for a successor just as OpenAI eyes a possible IPO, where she had been widely seen as a candidate for expanded responsibility. Her product and business duties will be divided among President Greg Brockman, CFO Sarah Friar, and Chief Strategy Officer Jason Kwon, with Chief Revenue Officer Denise Dresser now reporting to Brockman. *Sources: [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/09/fidji-simo-steps-down-from-openais-no-2-role/), [Variety](https://variety.com/2026/biz/news/fidji-simo-openai-step-down-recovery-illness-1236806169/), [The Next Web](https://thenextweb.com/news/fidji-simo-steps-down-from-openais-number-two-job).*

**6. China Weighs Limits on the AI Models American Companies Love** — Chinese officials are weighing restrictions on overseas access to the country's most advanced AI models, with the Ministry of Commerce leading meetings involving top firms including Alibaba, ByteDance, and X.ai to discuss limiting both closed-source and open-source models, per Reuters. Options reportedly sketched out include barring public release or restricting the models to domestic use only, with penalties for leaking or stealing such technology potentially treated as a national security offense. The move targets free Chinese models that U.S. businesses have increasingly adopted to cut costs versus proprietary American offerings: per OpenRouter data, weekly usage of Chinese AI models has stayed above 30% since February 8, peaking at 46%, up sharply from the prior year's 11% average. The restrictions would mirror U.S. export controls, with China now treating its cutting-edge AI as a national security asset requiring government control. *Sources: [TIME](https://time.com/article/2026/07/07/china-ai-models-alibaba-bytedance/), [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/07/08/china-mulls-limiting-foreign-access-advanced-ai-models/), [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/08/chinese-ai-models-probe-us-lawmakers.html).*

**1. The soft print stood in contrast to the broader tape, as the S&P 500 rose about 0.22% on July 9 and continued climbing into Friday, when it closed up 0.42% at 7,575.39. *Sources: [Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/articles/pepsico-q2-2026-earnings-revenue-121112189.html), [Benzinga](https://www.benzinga.com/news/26/07/60381507/these-analysts-slash-their-forecasts-on-pepsico-following-q2-results).*

## 💰 Personal Finance

**1. Circle Receives Approval to Launch Crypto-Focused Bank** — On July 10, 2026, USDC issuer Circle Internet Group (NYSE: CRCL) received final approval from the U.S. Office of the Comptroller of the Currency (OCC) to establish First National Digital Currency Bank, N.A., which will operate under the name Circle National Trust; the milestone follows Circle's application submitted June 30, 2025 and the conditional OCC approval it won in December 2025. The national trust bank will initially provide federally regulated fiduciary custody of digital assets for Circle and its affiliates, with plans to eventually offer custody to institutional clients and to bring management of the USDC reserve under direct OCC oversight. The approval strengthens the infrastructure behind USDC, the world's largest regulated stablecoin, whose market capitalization stood around $73 billion in early July 2026 and which posted roughly $1.21 trillion in adjusted transaction volume in June 2026, about double Tether's USDT. Circle shares rose sharply on the news (up roughly 5-14% intraday), extending momentum from its blockbuster NYSE debut in June 2025, when the stock priced at $31 per share and surged 168% on its first trading day. *Sources: [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/10/circle-gets-an-occ-bank-charter-as-stablecoin-competition-heats-up-shares-surge-14percent.html), [CoinDesk](https://www.coindesk.com/business/2026/07/10/circle-secures-u-s-trust-bank-approval-in-crypto-expansion), [Banking Dive](https://www.bankingdive.com/news/circle-occ-national-trust-bank-charter-full-approval-allaire-usdc-stablecoin/824969/).*
