**6. Are You Using the Wrong USB-C Charger? That Cheap Cable Can Fry Your Phone** — This consumer-tech piece warns that cheap, uncertified USB-C cables and chargers can damage phones because they often lack proper insulation, shielding, or a valid E-Marker chip, the component that tells a charger how much power the cable can safely carry. A counterfeit E-Marker may falsely claim a cable supports 100W when its wires can only handle around 30W, causing overheating that can melt plastic, damage USB-C port pins, or send a power spike into the phone's power-management IC. Regulators back up the risk: Health Canada found that 30 of 50 uncertified chargers it tested posed electric-shock or fire hazards. Delivering too much power to a device rated for less can also degrade its battery over time, even if the phone initially seems fine. Experts recommend sticking to reputable, certified brands for both the charging brick and the cable. *Sources: [How-To Geek](https://www.howtogeek.com/can-old-or-cheap-usb-cables-damage-your-devices/), [MakeUseOf](https://www.makeuseof.com/what-phone-charger-specs-mean/), [Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/using-wrong-usb-c-charger-090001043.html).*

**5. A Broken Elbow Forces Two Retirees to Ponder Their Future Mobility** — Falls are the leading cause of injury among seniors, and most occur at home, making an injury like a broken elbow a common trigger for retirees to reassess whether their homes can support aging in place. Research consistently shows home modifications—grab bars, non-slip mats, stair railings, widened doorways, better lighting, and stair lifts—effectively reduce fall incidence, especially in high-risk areas like bathrooms, and are most effective when combined with balance and strength exercise. Experts stress planning before a crisis hits, since larger projects can pay for themselves within a year by preventing even one fall, given that a single hospital stay can cost more than a full bathroom remodel plus a quality stair lift. Early planning also gives retirees time to compare product options and costs rather than making rushed decisions after an accident. *Sources: [Lifeway Mobility](https://www.lifewaymobility.com/blog/a-guide-to-aging-in-place-making-your-home-safe-accessible/), [NIH/PMC systematic review](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11988477/).*
